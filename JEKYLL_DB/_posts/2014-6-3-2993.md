The primary process extracts two kinds of raw products from both lemons and oranges, i.e., a water-oil emulsion derived from the citrus peels and juice from the pulp. Water is introduced into the emulsion to catch oil derived from the citrus peels, which would otherwise volatilize.\nTo remove impurities, peel scraps, and water content, the water-oil emulsion is refined (filtration process) and centrifuged twice. More than 90% of the removed water is reused in the primary extraction process, while the remaining water is transferred to a purification plant. Afterward, the refined oil is packaged and stored in refrigerating tanks at −5°C until ready to be distributed. Electricity is used for the filtration and centrifugation steps, the use of water pumps, packaging, and refrigeration.\nRaw juice from lemons and oranges extracted in the primary process is first refined and then pasteurized to remove the microbial and enzymatic potential from it. The pasteurizer is a plate heat exchanger, through which the raw juice (entering at 20–25°C and going out at 80°C) and steam (entering at 105°C and going out at about 80–100°C) flow in counter-current. Saturated steam is generated in a methane boiler. The pasteurization process takes about 15–20 s, and the juice is immediately cooled to 25°C. The speed of the pasteurization and cooling processes, together with the subsequent addition of antiseptics, aims to preserve the organoleptic properties of the juice.\nAbout 50% of the juice obtained is refrigerated further in tanks at 2°C, ready to be packed and promptly distributed to users as natural juice. The remaining 50% of juice is concentrated by a heat exchanger by vaporizing the water content using a steam boiler at 120°C. This has benefits in both financial and market terms. This product has lower costs for packaging, storage, and transportation, as well as less energy consumption than the refrigeration required by the equivalent amount of natural juice. In addition, the seasonal nature of the production system results in semimanufactured products being stored for a long period until needed. The concentrated juice is cooled, packaged in LDPE (low-density polyethylene) bags, and stored in tanks made of low-carbon steel.\n\n"
